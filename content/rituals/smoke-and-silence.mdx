Smoke teaches distance inside a small room. It rises, thins, and refuses to hurry — a visible metronome for attention.

Silence, here, is not emptiness. It is **permission** to hear wood tick, paper shift, breath deepen.

If the incense is good, it will not shout. It will occupy the middle air until you remember how much you were holding in your jaw.

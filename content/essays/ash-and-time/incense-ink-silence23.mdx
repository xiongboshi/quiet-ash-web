<EssayLead>Tools change weekly; habits should not.</EssayLead>

Here is a simple workflow that keeps quality high without turning the author into a passenger.

## Keep the human in the loop

Use models for *drafts* and *variants*, not for final judgment. Your taste is the product.

## Version everything

When a paragraph changes, keep the old version somewhere. Future-you will want to compare tone, not chase ghosts.

## Small prompts, tight constraints

<MarginNote>Constraints reduce rework</MarginNote>

```text
Role: editor
Task: shorten to 120 words
Tone: calm, direct
Output: markdown only
```

## When to stop

If the text reads smooth but says nothing, delete and restart with a sharper question. **Speed is not the goal — comprehension is.**
